## Review Log

### April 2026 — One Month On

**Mines** — Still the most underpriced tail risk. Nothing has changed structurally; there's no easy way to clear them. Holds.

**Inflation** — Still holds, I'd say it's a good read rather than a clean model — I thought it could go higher. The direction was right.

**Stagflation** — Still the base case. No reason to revise.

**Qatar LNG** — Over-reactionary. Prices are up but not catastrophically so.

**Iran** — Iran has pushed hard on the toll angle, which has put Gulf neighbours in a difficult position. The US responded by blocking passage of any tanker that paid Iranian fees — legally questionable, practically bad press as well. A lot of factors are moving against the US here. In terms of net wins and losses, Iran is probably winning the positioning game.

**Ceasefire** — Came faster than I expected. Trump declared victory and left, TACO. Israel used the window to move on Lebanon.

**Oil price** — Peaked near $118–119, now sitting around $95–97. Demand destruction hit harder and faster. Slow steaming, fuel switching, industrial cutbacks, Gulf bypasses. I was expecting a firmer $100+ floor if disruptions persisted and we're already drifting toward the $80 norm faster than anticipated. Not sure if that's markets pricing in the new equilibrium or expecting the disruption to just continue, still have prolonged sensitivity.

**What I missed** — Should have talked more about SEA and Europe absorbing the most pain. That was an obvious follow-on I left on the table.

### June 2026

Mostly still holds. The largest area where I got it wrong was the immediate stagflation. AI capex is running wild in sectors with low oil intensity. Earnings are through the roof. Not much else to say and I think this is the last update I'll do here. Most things have came to fruition.
